================================================================================
ACME CORPORATION - DATA RETENTION AND DISPOSAL POLICY
Version 2.1 | Effective Date: January 1, 2025
================================================================================

SECTION 1: PURPOSE AND SCOPE
================================================================================
This policy establishes requirements for the retention, archival, and secure
disposal of corporate data. It applies to all employees, contractors, and
third-party vendors who handle company data.

SECTION 2: DATA RETENTION SCHEDULES
================================================================================
Financial records, including invoices, receipts, and general ledger entries,
must be retained for a minimum of 7 years in accordance with IRS regulations.
Payroll records must be retained for 4 years after the tax year to which they
relate. Contracts and legal agreements must be retained for 10 years after
expiration or termination.

Employee records must be retained for 7 years after the employee's termination
date. Hiring and interview records for candidates who were not hired must be
retained for 2 years. Performance review records must be retained for the
duration of employment plus 5 years.

Customer data and transaction logs must be retained for 5 years from the date
of the last transaction. Support tickets and customer communications must be
retained for 3 years.

SECTION 3: ARCHIVAL REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================
Data older than 2 years that must be retained should be moved to cold storage
(AWS S3 Glacier or equivalent). Archived data must remain accessible within
72 hours of a retrieval request. Archived data must be indexed and tagged with
retention expiry dates to enable automated purging.

SECTION 4: SECURE DATA DISPOSAL
================================================================================
Electronic media containing sensitive or confidential data must be wiped using
DoD 5220.22-M standard (3-pass overwrite) or physically destroyed before
disposal. Hard drives from decommissioned servers must be degaussed and then
shredded by a certified destruction vendor.

Paper documents classified as Confidential or above must be shredded using a
cross-cut or micro-cut shredder. Shredding must be performed by an approved
vendor and a certificate of destruction must be obtained and filed.

Cloud data that has passed its retention period must be deleted using the
cloud provider's secure delete API, not standard delete. Deletion must be
confirmed with an audit log entry.

SECTION 5: LEGAL HOLDS
================================================================================
When litigation, regulatory investigation, or audit is reasonably anticipated,
a legal hold must be placed on all relevant data. Legal holds override standard
retention schedules and deletion processes. The Legal department is responsible
for issuing and lifting legal holds. IT must implement a technical hold within
24 hours of notification.

SECTION 6: COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT
================================================================================
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including
termination. Intentional destruction of records subject to a legal hold may
constitute obstruction of justice and result in criminal liability.

All employees must complete data retention training annually. Compliance with
this policy is audited quarterly by the Information Governance team.
================================================================================
END OF POLICY
